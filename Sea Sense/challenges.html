SDG14: Life Below Water and Its Challenges

Pollution

Oil spills, chemical runoff, and plastic waste pose a serious threat to marine ecosystems, making marine
pollution a serious problem. Because plastic pollution entangles and contaminates wildlife and enters the food
chain, it is especially worrying.

Overfishing

Fish stocks are being depleted due to unsustainable fishing methods, upsetting the delicate balance of marine
food webs. In addition, overfishing harms other important habitats like coral reefs.

Climate Change and Habitat Loss

Marine life is being severely impacted by the rising water temperatures, acidity, and sea level rise brought on
by climate change. Due to warmer waters, coral reefs are especially vulnerable, experiencing bleaching and
eventually dying.Seagrass beds, mangroves, and coral reefs are just a few of the essential marine habitats
that are disappearing due to pollution, coastal development, and harmful fishing methods. Numerous maritime
species depend on these areas for their eating, nesting, and breeding needs.

Lack of funds

SDG 14 cannot be implemented successfully without substantial financial resources and robust enforcement
protocols. The quantity of money required for marine conservation initiatives and what is now available fall
short of each other. Furthermore, many areas still struggle with implementing laws against pollution and
damaging fishing methods.

Lack of Awareness

Encouraging change requires raising public awareness of the grave condition of our seas and the need for
immediate action. It's possible that many individuals are unaware of the connection between human welfare
and ocean health.
